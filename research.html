Research

Behavioral Science of Data Visualization

I study how people respond to data visualizations. My research looks at how chart design can shape what people notice, how they interpret information, and how their perceptions, attitudes, and online behavior respond to what they see.

I focus especially on visual complexity and uncertainty displays, asking when charts make evidence easier or harder to process, trust, and use for belief updating.
