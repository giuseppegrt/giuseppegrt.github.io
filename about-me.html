About me

A little context beyond the CV

Bio

I’m Giuseppe, an Italian scholar based in Switzerland, where I moved for my PhD. Most of my path has unfolded inside universities: first as a student, then through research, teaching, and the quieter service work that helps academic communities function. Moving between places and institutions has taught me to pay attention to context, to listen before drawing conclusions, and to enjoy the slow process of understanding a new environment from the inside.

My training is in economics, and my research now sits in the behavioral sciences, but my curiosity rarely stays inside one disciplinary border. I read often, sometimes philosophically and often across languages, because I am drawn to questions that take time to unfold. In my work, that means studying problems that feel relevant beyond the moment and accepting that good answers often arrive incrementally.

Right now I’m focused on finishing my PhD. Starting 1 April 2026, I’m open to scientific roles both inside and outside academia. Away from work, I’m an experimental cook, a traveler when schedules allow, and a devoted listener of good music. This page gathers the more personal side of that story: a few links, a little context, and some playlists for anyone who feels like listening.
